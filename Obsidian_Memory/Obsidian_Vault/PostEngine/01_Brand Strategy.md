# PostEngine — Brand Strategy

---

## Positioning

**Category:** Social media management  
**For:** Small UK business owners (start: trades + construction)  
**Against:** Metricool, Buffer, Later — generic tools that don't speak your language  
**What makes it different:** AI writes it. You approve it. It posts it. Built for your trade.

**Positioning statement:**  
*"The social media tool that already knows what to say."*

---

## Name Options

Working name: **PostEngine**

Other candidates:
- **Drafter** — suggests AI writing, clean
- **Postcraft** — trade feel, craft = quality
- **Dispatch** — sends content out, trade-adjacent language
- **Forge** — heavy, trade, making things (conflicts with ForgeOS in vault?)
- **Sendcraft** — clean, action-oriented
- **Patch** — territory + posting (ties to JobFilter brand language)

**Decision needed:** Pick before any public presence is built.

---

## Visual Identity Direction

Follow the same brutalist-but-premium direction as JobFilter — but slightly cleaner for a software product.

**Palette:**
- Primary: `#0A0A0A` (black)
- Accent: `#E3B72A` (yellow) — same family as JobFilter
- Background: `#FFFDF4` (paper)
- Success: `#22C55E` (green)
- Alert: `#C5462A` (orange)

**Why same palette as JobFilter:** Brand family. Same founder. Signals consistency. And it's a proven differentiation from the blue/purple SaaS gradient world.

**Logo direction:**
- Wordmark: `POSTENGINE` or chosen name in Barlow Condensed ExtraBold
- Icon: abstract "P" or arrow-in-box mark — simple, works at 16px
- No rounded corners. No gradients. No pastel.

**Typography:**
- Barlow Condensed (headlines)
- Barlow (body)
- JetBrains Mono (data/labels)

---

## Voice & Tone

Same rules as JobFilter — but more product-focused.

**Personality:**
- Direct
- Confident
- Slightly impatient (mirrors the user who doesn't have time)
- Never corporate. Never startup-jargon.

**Tone in practice:**
- Bad: "Leverage AI-powered content automation to optimise your social media ROI"
- Good: "Tell it what you want to say. It writes it. You post it."

**Phrases to use:**
- "already written"
- "approved and gone"
- "no blank page"
- "done before your next job"
- "one less thing"

---

## Brand Promise

**You should never have to think about what to post.**

The tool knows your business, your audience, your voice. You approve, tweak, or skip. It does the rest.
