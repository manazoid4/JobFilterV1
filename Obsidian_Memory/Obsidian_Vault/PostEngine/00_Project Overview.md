# PostEngine — Project Overview

> **One-liner:** The social media OS for small businesses that don't have a marketing team.

**Status:** Idea stage — 2026-05-19  
**Founder:** Solo  
**Model:** SaaS, monthly subscription  
**Inspiration:** Metricool — but niche-first, AI-native, and built for people who run a business, not a marketing agency.

---

## What Is This

A social media management platform. Like Metricool or Buffer — but:

1. **Niche-first.** Start with one vertical (UK trades / small construction businesses) and own it before going wide. Metricool is for everyone. That's also its weakness — it doesn't speak any one customer's language.
2. **AI-native from day one.** Not AI as a bolt-on feature ("click to rewrite"). AI as the engine — you tell it what you want to say, it writes, schedules, adapts per platform, and posts.
3. **Dead simple.** Metricool has 40+ features most users never touch. PostEngine does 5 things extremely well.

---

## The Problem It Solves

Most small business owners know they should post on social media. They never do because:

- Writing copy takes time they don't have
- They don't know what to post
- Switching between platforms is annoying
- Scheduling tools are built for agencies, not sole traders

**The result:** Inconsistent or zero social presence. Lost work.

---

## The Opportunity

- Metricool: ~$30M ARR, ~300k users, serving everyone
- Buffer: ~$20M ARR, similar
- Neither is built for a specific niche
- Neither has deep AI integration (it's a checkbox feature, not the core)
- Neither speaks trade language, contractor language, or small UK business language

A niche-specific version with real AI at the core = defensible wedge.

---

## Ground Rules

1. **Niche first, horizontal second.** Launch for trades/construction. Expand to other verticals (hospitality, health, retail) only after £10k MRR in niche.
2. **AI is the product, not a feature.** Users should never have to write a post from scratch.
3. **No agency features in v1.** No white-labelling, no team seats, no client dashboards — that's Hootsuite's market.
4. **Revenue before features.** First paying customer before building feature #2.
5. **Validate with JobFilter users first.** They're already the target market.
