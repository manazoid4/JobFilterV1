# 30-Day Launch Plan

Low budget. High intention. Validate before you spend.

---

## Guiding Principle

Do not spend on manufacturing until you have 20 real people who want to buy.  
Do not spend on design until you have a formula worth designing around.  
Do not spend on ads until you have something organic that converts.

Prove, then invest. Every time.

---

## WEEK 1 — Research, Name, Formula, Compliance

### Daily Actions

**Day 1 — Name Decision**
- Decide: Fitrah as brand name, Rumman as product name
- Check: fitrah.co.uk domain availability (and .com)
- Search: "Fitrah" UK trademark register (gov.uk — free)
- Decision: Lock name only if domain + trademark are clear

**Day 2 — Formula Test 1**
- Buy: pomegranate juice (100% pure, Waitrose/Asda/Amazon), beetroot juice (cold pressed), coconut water, lemon, sea salt
- Make: Basic 500ml test formula (see 03_First_Product.md)
- Taste: Rate 1–10. Adjust ratios. Document everything.
- Share: Give to 2–3 honest people. Get real feedback. Write it down.

**Day 3 — Compliance Check**
- Read 04_Compliance_Risks.md
- Book Level 2 Food Hygiene Certificate (free online — do it now)
- Note: Do NOT sell anything until you have this
- Research: Local council food business registration process

**Day 4 — Formula Test 2**
- Adjust based on Day 2 feedback
- Try with ginger (grate, not powder)
- Try with a tiny amount of raw honey
- Try a more concentrated shot version (60–100ml)
- Photograph every version

**Day 5 — Competitor Check**
- Search: "beetroot shot UK", "pomegranate drink UK", "mineral hydration UK", "halal electrolyte drink UK"
- Note: gaps (already identified in 06_Competitor_Research.md)
- Visit: Beet It, LMNT, Precision Hydration websites — understand their positioning

**Day 6 — Brand Identity (MVP)**
- Create: "FITRAH" wordmark in Canva (Barlow Condensed Black, white on black)
- Create: Simple Instagram profile image
- Write: Brand bio (from 10_Copy_Bank.md)
- Do NOT commission a logo yet

**Day 7 — Rest / Review**
- Review what you made. What tastes good. What needs changing.
- Write 2–3 lines about why you're building this.

## WEEK 2 — Brand, Waitlist, Quiz, Content Start

### What to post this week
- Post 1: Founder story — "I'm building a real mineral drink. Here's why."
- Post 2: Ingredient education — "Pomegranate and beetroot: what they actually do."
- Post 3: Taste test video — honest reaction, first formula
- Post 4: Behind-the-scenes — buying ingredients, making the first batch

### Build the quiz
- Platform: Tally.so (free) or Typeform (free tier)
- Quiz: Mineral Deficiency Risk Checker (8 questions)
- Result: Score + email capture
- Email tool: Mailchimp or ConvertKit free
- Landing page for quiz: Carrd.co (free or £15/year)

### Build the waitlist page
- Simple one-page site: Carrd or Framer
- Headline: "The Red Mineral Tonic. First batch coming."
- Email capture: "Join the founder list"
- What they get: early access, locked price, behind-the-scenes
- No payment yet — just emails

### What to measure
- Instagram/TikTok followers gained
- Emails captured from quiz or waitlist
- Open rates on emails
- Real formula feedback from taste tests

### What NOT to spend money on
- Ads
- Custom labels
- Bottle stock
- Logo design
- Website beyond a basic Carrd page

## WEEK 3 — Sampling, Feedback, Founder Offer

### Sampling plan
- Make a fresh batch (50 bottles if possible)
- Label simply: "FITRAH RUMMAN — Taste Test Edition"
- Give to: gym members, friends, family, local community (mosque, sports club)
- Ask for honest 1–10 feedback on: taste, colour, texture, would you buy, what would you pay
- Document all feedback

### Founder offer launch
- Write the Founder 50 page (one page on Carrd or Notion)
- What they get (see 07_Money_Making_Strategy.md)
- Price: £29 pre-order credit or £49 Founder Challenge
- Payment: Stripe (easy setup, free to create)
- Share: to your Instagram, waitlist email, WhatsApp

### Email sequence (set up in Week 2, now send)
- Email 1 sent (welcome + quiz result)
- Email 2 sent (why real food beats supplements)
- Email 3 sent (the Red Tonic story)
- Email 4: Founder offer reveal

### Formula refinement
- Based on sampling feedback: adjust salt level, sweetness, concentration
- Aim: formula that gets 7+/10 from 80% of tasters

### What to post this week
- Post 5: Sampling day — give-out video
- Post 6: Raw honest feedback reel (show real people trying it)
- Post 7: "Founder 50 is now open"
- Post 8: Behind-the-scenes of formula refinement

## WEEK 4 — Founder Launch, Digital Product, Decision Point

### Digital product launch
- Create: Mineral Hydration Guide PDF (£9) in Canva
- Create: Simple Gumroad or Lemon Squeezy product page (free)
- Email: Send to waitlist — "Our first digital product is ready"
- Post: Announce on social

### Founder Waitlist push
- Email the list again with founder offer
- "We close Founder 50 applications this week"
- Post every day this week about it

### Measure and decide
At end of Week 4, answer:
- How many founder signups? (Target: 20+)
- How many digital product sales? (Target: 10+)
- How many bottles given away and what score? (Target: avg 7+/10)
- How many emails captured? (Target: 200+)

### Decision rules
- **Green light to manufacture:** 30+ genuine interest signals (signups, paid digital, pre-orders), avg taste score 7+/10
- **Amber — reformulate and retest:** Good interest, but taste feedback below 7. Keep selling digital.
- **Red — rethink:** Low interest AND taste issues. Go back to positioning. The product isn't connecting yet.
