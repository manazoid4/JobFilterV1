# Visual Identity

---

## 3 Visual Directions

---

### Direction 1 — Red Mineral Warrior

**Concept:** Dark and strong. Deep red and black. The drink is the weapon.

**Colours:**
- Primary: Deep red / pomegranate red (`#8B1A1A` or `#C0392B`)
- Secondary: Near-black (`#0D0D0D`)
- Accent: Raw off-white / bone (`#F5F0E8`)
- Mineral touch: Dark brass/copper (`#9C7A3C`)

**Fonts:**
- Headline: Something condensed and bold — similar to Barlow Condensed (used in JobFilter), or Tungsten, Oswald Black
- Body: Clean sans-serif — Inter, DM Sans
- Detail/label: Mono or small caps

**Logo idea:** Simple wordmark in all-caps. "FITRAH" in heavy condensed letters. Optional icon: a pomegranate silhouette or abstract mineral crystal shape.

**Label style:** Dark label. Product name large. Ingredients minimal and readable. Strong brand mark. Clean but not clinical. No gradients, no soft edges. Hard lines and clear hierarchy.

**Bottle style:** Dark glass (amber or dark green) or matte black/dark HDPE. Premium feel without fake luxury. Cylinder with embossed brand mark is ideal long-term.

**Social media style:** Dark backgrounds. Red-toned product photography. Short punchy copy. Strong contrast. No emoji. No pastel anything.

**Pros:** Strong shelf presence. Premium. Masculine. Memorable. Unique in the category.
**Cons:** Can feel intimidating to some. Dark bottles cost more than clear.

---

### Direction 2 — Ancient Apothecary

**Concept:** Heritage. Timeless. The feeling of a remedy that has existed for centuries.

**Colours:**
- Primary: Deep forest green (`#2D4A33`) or dark teal (`#1A3D3A`)
- Secondary: Parchment / warm cream (`#F0E8D5`)
- Accent: Pomegranate red (`#A83232`)
- Gold: Soft brass (`#B5973A`)

**Fonts:**
- Headline: Serif — Freight Display, Canela, or a strong serif like Playfair Display Black
- Body: Clean serif or transitional serif
- Label: Small italic serif for ingredient notes

**Logo idea:** A pomegranate illustration (detailed, apothecary-style) with FITRAH wordmark beneath in serif. Optional: pomegranate cross-section as icon.

**Label style:** Cream label with green and red accents. Illustrated elements. Feels like something found in an old spice market. Story-heavy label with space for founder's note on reverse.

**Bottle style:** Amber glass. Cork or matte cap. 250ml shot or 500ml bottle.

**Social media style:** Warm tones. Earthy product shots. Flat lay with real ingredients. Pomegranate, beetroot, salt, lemon arranged around the bottle.

**Pros:** Instantly communicates "real food." Premium. Excellent for storytelling content. Unique and giftable.
**Cons:** Might read as feminine or soft to some male buyers. Harder to produce affordably at MVP stage.

---

### Direction 3 — Premium Black/Gold Strength ⭐ RECOMMENDED

**Concept:** Clean. Powerful. Premium. The intersection of faith, discipline and performance — without cringe.

**Colours:**
- Primary: Flat black (`#080808` — same as JobFilter's --ink)
- Secondary: Deep pomegranate/garnet red (`#8C1C13`)
- Accent: Warm gold / deep yellow (`#C9973A` — richer than DeWalt yellow)
- Neutral: Stone grey (`#2C2C2C`) and off-white (`#F4F1EB`)

**Fonts:**
- Headline: Barlow Condensed Black or similar — strong, condensed, masculine
- Body: Clean geometric sans — Barlow, Inter
- Label: Mono or small caps for ingredient/detail text

**Logo idea:** "FITRAH" in strong all-caps condensed lettering. Optionally paired with a minimal pomegranate/seed motif (one seed, not an illustration). Or a simple geometric mark (hexagon, diamond, circle with inner mark).

**Label style:**
- Front: Black label. Brand name large. Product name (RUMMAN) below. Minimal. Strong.
- Back: Ingredient story. "Made with pomegranate, beetroot, sea salt, lemon." "No fake stimulants. No synthetic colours. No nonsense."
- Compliance text small but complete.

**Bottle style:** 330ml or 500ml dark glass or matte black aluminium can (long term). For MVP: clear glass with dark label is cheaper and easier.

**Social media style:** Black backgrounds. Bold typography. Real photography of ingredients + bottle. Short, punchy captions. Works on Instagram and TikTok equally.

**Pros:**
- Most versatile
- Works for Muslim audience, gym audience, and tradesman audience
- Premium without being expensive to execute
- Scales from MVP to retail without changing the identity
- Looks credible immediately
- Closest to the brand's values: strong, clean, grounded, not soft

**Cons:** Black/gold is used by some brands — must execute well to differentiate.

---

## Decision: Direction 3

**Black + Pomegranate Red + Warm Gold**

This direction wins because:
- It's the most versatile across all 3 audience types
- It's affordable to execute even at MVP stage
- It doesn't alienate any audience
- It reads as premium without fake luxury
- It signals strength without cringe
- It can grow from a label on a simple bottle to premium glass and eventually cans

---

## MVP Visual Execution (Zero Budget)

1. **Logo:** Canva Pro or free — FITRAH in Barlow Condensed Bold, all caps. Black text on white or white text on black.
2. **Label:** Canva or Affinity Publisher. Black background. Product name. Ingredient list. Compliance text.
3. **Social:** iPhone/Android photos with consistent black/dark background. Natural lighting. Real ingredients.
4. **Website:** Framer or Webflow (free tier) or simple Carrd. Dark theme. Strong headline.

Do not pay a designer until you've validated the product with real buyers.

## Packaging Notes (Future)

- MVP: Printed labels on clear glass bottles (~£0.05–£0.20 per label)
- Phase 2: Custom printed labels on dark glass (~£0.50–£1.50 per bottle minimum run)
- Phase 3: Embossed or screenprinted glass, or custom aluminium cans (minimum run 5,000–10,000 units, requires co-packer)
- For digital products: PDF covers, landing page graphics only needed
