# Compliance Risks — UK Food & Drink Law

**DISCLAIMER: This is a research summary for planning. Always verify with a food safety solicitor or the FSA before selling anything commercially.**

---

## 1. Food Business Registration

**What:** Any food business must register with their local council.
**When:** At least 28 days before starting to trade.
**Cost:** Free.
**Link:** gov.uk — Register a food business
**Action needed:** Register as soon as you decide to sell.

---

## 2. Labelling Requirements (Pre-packaged drinks)

Every packaged drink sold in the UK must show:
- Product name
- Ingredients list (in descending weight order)
- Net quantity (ml)
- Best before / use by date
- Storage conditions
- Name and address of manufacturer or seller
- Country of origin (if required)
- Allergen information (bold in ingredients list)
- Nutrition declaration (per 100ml): energy, fat, saturated fat, carbs, sugars, protein, salt

**Minimum label requirement is not optional — you cannot legally sell without it.**

## 3. Allergens

The 14 major allergens must be clearly declared.  
For the MVP red tonic: check if any of these are present:
- Celery — no
- Gluten/wheat — no (unless mixed in facility)
- Peanuts — no
- Tree nuts — no
- Soya — check coconut water brands
- Milk — no (no dairy in this product)
- Eggs — no
- Mustard — no
- Sesame — no
- Fish/shellfish/molluscs — no
- Lupin — no
- Sulphites >10mg/kg — check if present in any ingredient

**For the MVP tonic, allergen risk is very low.**  
Add a "Made in a facility that handles [X]" statement if making in a shared kitchen.

## 4. Health and Nutrition Claims

This is the MOST IMPORTANT compliance area for this brand.

### UK Approved Claims Only

The UK Retained Regulation on Nutrition and Health Claims sets out what you can and cannot say.  
Only claims on the approved GB register are legal.

**SAFE claims for juice-based drinks (generally acceptable):**
- "Source of vitamin C" (if pomegranate content qualifies)
- "Contains potassium" (if >15% RI per 100ml)
- "Electrolyte drink"
- "Mineral hydration"
- "Made with pomegranate and beetroot juice"
- "No artificial colours"
- "No artificial sweeteners"
- "No preservatives" (if true)
- "Vegan" / "Plant-based"
- "Halal-suitable" (if ingredients verified)

**BORDERLINE (need specific approval or qualification):**
- "Supports normal muscle function" — potassium claim, approved if potassium content qualifies
- "Contributes to normal energy metabolism" — magnesium, if content qualifies

**FORBIDDEN / AVOID:**
- "Boosts testosterone"
- "Improves blood pressure"
- "Improves blood flow" (implied medical claim)
- "Treats fatigue"
- "Fixes hormones"
- "Detoxes"
- "Clinically proven" (unless you have a real clinical trial)
- "Reduces inflammation" (medical claim)
- "Supports erectile function"
- "Increases nitric oxide" (implied medical/physiological claim)
- "Improves athletic performance" (borderline — needs approved claim wording)

**SAFE MARKETING LANGUAGE (use these):**
- "Mineral hydration for men who train"
- "Made from real food"
- "Simple ingredients you can read"
- "Training-friendly hydration"
- "No fake stimulants. No crash."
- "Whole-food based"
- "Halal-conscious. Real ingredients."
- "Your ancestors didn't drink neon."
- "Built for active men"
- "Part of a disciplined routine"

## 5. Selling Bottled Drinks

For home/small kitchen production:
- You must operate from a registered food premises
- May need a Food Hygiene Certificate (Level 2 at minimum — free online courses available)
- Third-party manufacturing (co-packer) is cleanest for scale — they handle most food safety

For e-commerce (UK):
- Distance selling regulations apply
- Must have clear returns policy
- Must display allergens and nutrition info online before purchase

For market stalls / sampling:
- Temporary food premise registration may be needed
- Check local council rules
- Great for validation before full manufacturing

---

## 6. Raw Milk — Clear Warning

**Do not sell raw milk commercially in the UK (other than direct from farm).**

UK law: Raw milk can only be sold direct to consumer at the farm gate or at farmers markets (from registered raw milk producers). It cannot be sold in shops, online, or through third parties in England, Wales and Northern Ireland.  
Scotland: Raw milk sale is banned entirely.

**Risk if ignored:** Criminal prosecution, FSA enforcement, product recall, brand destruction before you start.

**Brand strategy:** Talk about raw milk as a philosophy/content topic. Promote the idea of real, unprocessed food. Do not sell it.

---

## 7. Supplement vs. Food Drink

**Key distinction:** If you add vitamins, minerals or botanicals at therapeutic levels, it may be classified as a food supplement, which has additional regulations.

**For the MVP tonic, stay firmly in "food drink" territory:**
- Use sea salt, not sodium chloride supplement
- Use cream of tartar for potassium, not potassium supplement tablets
- If adding magnesium, keep amounts low (within normal food intake)
- Do not add anything that requires a Novel Food authorisation

**Novel Foods:** Certain ingredients not widely used in the EU before May 1997 need Novel Food authorisation. Check the Novel Food catalogue (food.gov.uk) before adding any botanical extracts.  
- Pomegranate juice: safe
- Beetroot juice: safe
- Ashwagandha extract: NOVEL FOOD — do not add without authorisation
- CBD: Novel Food
- Most standard juices and electrolytes: safe

---

## 8. Halal Considerations

For plant-based drinks:
- No animal-derived ingredients (gelatin, carmine, etc.) → halal by nature
- Check co-packers use halal-compliant facilities if you care about cross-contamination
- Honey: halal, but check source
- Apple cider vinegar: permissible (not alcohol — transformed into acid)

**Safest position:** "All ingredients are plant-based and halal-suitable. No alcohol-based extracts or haram additives."

## Safest MVP Route Summary

1. Make small batches in registered kitchen (home registration or shared food space)
2. Use only standard food ingredients (juice, salt, lemon, coconut water)
3. Create simple labels with all required information
4. Use only FSA/GB approved claims or no claims at all
5. Sell via sampling → founder waitlist → pre-orders → small batch
6. No therapeutic claims. No supplement territory.
7. No raw milk product.
8. Get a Level 2 Food Hygiene Certificate before selling.
9. Consult a food safety specialist before scaling.
