# Field Notes Content System

## Philosophy

JobFilter needs **human conviction** without requiring founder selfies or influencer branding.

Field Notes are tactical insights written in the voice of:
- A foreman
- An intelligence analyst
- An operator
- A tactical construction insider

**NOT:**
- Generic blogs
- SEO spam
- Startup cringe
- Fake motivational content

---

## Tone of Voice

### Characteristics
- Direct. No fluff.
- Uses tradesman language.
- Assumes the reader is smart but busy.
- No exclamation marks unless genuinely excited.
- No emojis.
- Data-backed claims.
- Personal experience where relevant.

### Example Opening Lines
- "The real cost of a ghost quote is not the fuel. It's the hope."
- "Birmingham extension activity is quietly exploding. Here's the data."
- "Shared leads don't just cost money. They cost margin."
- "I spent three hours quoting a job that never existed. Here's how to avoid it."

### Words to Use
- Job, quote, site, waste, graft, chase, margin, fuel, time, signal, lead, patch, territory

### Words to Avoid
- AI-powered, machine learning, SaaS, platform, solution, ecosystem, leverage, synergise, optimise, disruptive, innovative, game-changing, revolutionary

---

## Content Categories

### 1. The Real Cost Series
**Format:** Personal story + data + actionable tip.

Examples:
- "The Real Cost Of Ghost Quotes"
- "The Real Cost Of Shared Leads"
- "The Real Cost Of Underpricing"
- "The Real Cost Of Driving To Time-Wasters"

### 2. Market Intelligence
**Format:** Data analysis + local insight + opportunity.

Examples:
- "Birmingham Extension Activity Is Quietly Exploding"
- "Why Manchester Roofers Are About To Be Flat Out"
- "The EPC Deadline No One Is Talking About"
- "Postcode B17: 14 Approvals In 30 Days"

### 3. Tactical Guides
**Format:** Short, actionable advice.

Examples:
- "How To Spot A Tyre-Kicker In 3 Questions"
- "The 10-Second Quote Floor"
- "When To Walk Away From A Job"
- "How To Price Fuel Into Every Quote"

### 4. Signal Spotlights
**Format:** Deep dive on one signal type.

Examples:
- "What Planning Data Actually Reveals"
- "Why EPC F Ratings Are A Gold Mine"
- "Council Contracts: How To Win Before The Tender Closes"
- "Land Registry: The Signal No One Else Sees"

### 5. Builder Psychology
**Format:** Behavioural insight + practical application.

Examples:
- "Why Good Builders Lose Work To Better Presentation"
- "The Homeowner's Mind: What They Really Want"
- "Why Urgency Beats Price Every Time"
- "The Follow-Up That Closes Jobs"

## Page Structure

### Field Notes Index (/field-notes)
- Grid of articles.
- Category filter tabs.
- Search by keyword.
- "Latest" and "Most Read" sections.

### Individual Article (/field-notes/:slug)
- Headline (H1)
- Category tag
- Date
- Read time (3-5 minutes)
- Lead image (optional — data visualisation preferred over stock photo)
- Body (short paragraphs, subheadings, bullet points)
- CTA box at bottom: "Scan your area" or "Try the free tools"
- Related articles

## Visual Design

### Typography
- Headlines: Barlow Condensed, uppercase, bold.
- Body: Barlow, regular weight.
- Data callouts: JetBrains Mono.

### Layout
- Single column, max-width 680px.
- Generous whitespace.
- Pull quotes in yellow boxes.
- Data tables where relevant.
- No sidebar distractions.

### Images
- Data visualisations (charts, maps).
- Screenshot of signal examples (anonymised).
- Minimal. No stock photos of builders shaking hands.

## SEO Strategy (Minimal)

Target long-tail, low-competition keywords:
- "how to avoid tyre kickers construction"
- "planning data for builders"
- "ghost leads tradesmen"
- "how to price quotes correctly"

**Do NOT optimise for:**
- "lead generation"
- "construction software"
- Generic SaaS terms.

## Example Article

**Title:** The Real Cost Of Ghost Quotes
**Category:** The Real Cost Series
**Read time:** 4 minutes

**Opening:**
> "I drove to Harborne last Tuesday. Forty minutes there, forty back. £18 in diesel. Two hours writing the quote. Sent it by email.
> 
> Never heard from him again.
> 
> That quote cost me £87 and half a day. I made zero. And I am not alone."

**Body:**
- Data: Average ghost rate is 30-50%.
- Data: Average wasted visit costs £25-50.
- Story: The emotional toll (hope → effort → silence → anger).
- Analysis: Why homeowners ghost (price shopping, not ready, change of mind).
- Solution: How to spot ghosts early (planning data, property signals, budget indicators).

**Closing:**
> "The best way to avoid a ghost is to never quote for one in the first place."

**CTA:**
> "Try the Tyre-Kicker Check — free. Score a lead before you waste a visit."
