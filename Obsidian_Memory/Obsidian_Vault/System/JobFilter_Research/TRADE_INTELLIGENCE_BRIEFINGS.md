# Trade Intelligence Briefings

## What Is a Trade Intelligence Briefing?

A **weekly tactical report** sent to members every Monday morning.

Not a newsletter. Not a sales email. An **intelligence briefing**.

> "What happened in your patch last week. What's opening this week. What to chase first."

---

## Format

### Subject Line
"Trade Intel — Week of {date}: 14 signals in B17, 3 new approvals"

### Structure

**1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (3 bullets)**
- Total signals in your territory this week.
- Biggest opportunity.
- Trend (up/down/flat).

**2. SIGNAL BREAKDOWN**
| Signal Type | Count | Trend | Top Opportunity |
|-------------|-------|-------|-----------------|
| Planning | 8 | ↑ | 4-bed extension, B17 |
| EPC | 3 | → | F-rated rental, B15 |
| Contracts | 2 | ↑ | School maintenance tender |
| Land Registry | 1 | ↓ | — |

**3. HOT LEAD SPOTLIGHT**
One lead with full detail:
- What it is.
- Why it's hot.
- Recommended action.
- Timing.

**4. TERRITORY TREND**
- Score change this week.
- Comparison to last week.
- Prediction for next week.

**5. MARKET NOTE**
One paragraph on local market conditions:
- "Birmingham South is seeing a spike in extension approvals."
- "Manchester contracts are slowing ahead of summer."
- "Retrofit grant deadline approaching in your area."

**6. ACTION LIST**
- [ ] Call {lead} — planning approved 2 days ago
- [ ] Quote {lead} — EPC F rating, landlord motivated
- [ ] Watch {postcode} — 3 signals this week, may open up

**7. TOOL TIP**
One quick tip:
- "Use the Quote Floor before calling {lead}. Budget is £45k, your floor is £38k."

---

## Delivery

### Primary Channel
- WhatsApp Business (text + image/pdf attachment)
- Format: Short paragraphs, bullet points, one image per week.

### Secondary Channel
- Email (HTML + plain text)
- Format: Same content, longer form allowed.

### Timing
- Every Monday, 6:00 AM.
- Before tradesmen start their week.
- Not Friday (forgotten by Monday).
- Not daily (overwhelming).

## Implementation

### Phase 1 (Manual)
- Manually compile briefing every Monday.
- Send via WhatsApp and email.
- Template in Google Docs or Notion.

### Phase 2 (Semi-Automated)
- Automated data pulls.
- Manual curation of hot lead and market note.
- Template populated automatically.

### Phase 3 (Fully Automated)
- Fully automated generation.
- AI-assisted market note writing.
- Human review before send.
