## Category 1 — Electrolyte Drinks

### LMNT
- **What:** Electrolyte sachets (sodium, potassium, magnesium). Paleo/keto friendly.
- **Price:** ~£2.50–£3 per sachet
- **Claims:** Hydration, performance, no sugar
- **Visual:** Clean, minimalist, slightly medical
- **Weakness:** American-first brand, expensive for UK, no cultural alignment, no food story
- **Gap:** No ancestral/halal angle. No real food story. No UK community.
- **How we win:** Fitrah has a story LMNT doesn't. Real food ingredients (juice, not just powder). Community angle.

### Precision Hydration
- **What:** Electrolyte tablets/sachets for athletes
- **Price:** ~£1.50–£2.50 per sachet
- **Claims:** Sports performance, sweat rate matching
- **Visual:** Clinical, sports-lab style
- **Weakness:** Sterile, no personality, science-first but no philosophy
- **Gap:** Zero cultural relevance. No masculine lifestyle angle.
- **How we win:** Brand story and community that PH can't replicate.

### Lucozade Sport / Gatorade
- **What:** Mass market electrolyte drinks
- **Price:** £1–£2 per bottle
- **Claims:** Hydration, sports energy
- **Weakness:** High sugar, artificial colours, no premium angle, no credibility with health-conscious men
- **Gap:** The entire health-conscious segment
- **How we win:** We are the alternative. "Your ancestors didn't drink neon."

## Category 2 — Beetroot Shots

### Beet It (James White Drinks)
- **What:** Concentrated beetroot shot (70ml)
- **Price:** £1.80–£2.50 per shot
- **Claims:** "Used by elite athletes" — sports nutrition angle
- **Visual:** Clinical, not premium, not masculine
- **Weakness:** No lifestyle story. No community. No flavour innovation. Tastes medicinal.
- **Gap:** No masculine brand identity. No ancestral angle.
- **How we win:** Better flavour profile (pomegranate + beet + salt). Better branding. Better story. Community.

### Biotta Beetroot Juice
- **What:** Organic beetroot juice (500ml)
- **Price:** ~£3–£4 per bottle
- **Weakness:** European brand, no UK story, soft/organic positioning, no masculine angle
- **Gap:** Entirely open for a masculine UK beetroot brand
- **How we win:** UK, masculine, story-rich, halal-conscious, community-first

## Category 4 — Natural Pre-Workouts

### Innermost — Natural Pre-Workout
- **What:** Natural pre-workout powder

- **Gap:** Completely open for masculine natural performance brand.

## Category 5 — Ancestral Health Brands

### Wild Pastures / Primal Meats (UK)
- **What:** Grass-fed meat boxes
- **Price:** £60–£120/month subscription
- **Visual:** Dark, earthy, strong
- **Weakness:** Food only, no drinks, no community beyond email
- **Gap:** No drink companion product. No male health community.
- **Opportunity:** Partner or position alongside these brands.

### Ancestral Supplements (USA)
- **What:** Organ meat capsules — liver, kidney, heart
- **Price:** £30–£60 per bottle
- **Claims:** "Like consuming the whole animal"
- **Weakness:** USA-only, capsules (not food), hard to explain to general audience
- **Gap:** UK version with a food-first angle doesn't exist
- **Opportunity:** Fitrah fills the gap for UK men wanting ancestral nutrition without ordering from the US.

## Category 6 — Halal Wellness / Muslim Health

### Current state: Almost nothing exists.

- A few halal protein brands (generic)
- Some halal supplement resellers
- No authentic halal health lifestyle brand in the UK market
- Muslim men buy from generic brands and hope ingredients are clean

**This is the biggest gap in the market.**

A brand that authentically speaks to Muslim men about health, strength and food — without being preachy or compromising on quality — does not meaningfully exist in the UK.

**Fitrah can own this.**
