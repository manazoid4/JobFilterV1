# Audience — 3 Core Personas

---

## Persona 1 — The Muslim Man Rebuilding

**Name:** Adam, 27  
**Location:** Birmingham, Manchester, London  
**Age:** 22–38  
**Lifestyle:** Practising Muslim, works in finance/tech/trades, gym 3–4x per week or wants to start, recently started caring about what he eats. Post-Ramadan reset mentality.

### Pain Points
- Doesn't want haram ingredients but halal food options are usually junk
- Tired of whey protein that lists 40 ingredients
- Knows he should eat better but doesn't know where to start
- Energy crashes during the day (desk job or physical work)
- Wants to look strong, feel disciplined, and align his health with his deen
- Has guilt about bad eating habits — wants to reset

### Currently Buys
- Lucozade Sport, Gatorade, Monster occasionally
- Standard protein powder (may worry about halal status)
- Wholefoods/Holland & Barrett products sometimes
- Dates, honey, pomegranate — culturally familiar

### Why They'd Buy Fitrah
- "This is halal, no dodgy ingredients, and it makes sense with my lifestyle"
- Brand speaks to him without lecturing him
- The Fitrah name/concept resonates deeply
- He wants real food, not a supplement stack
- It fits his morning routine / workout prep

### Objections
- "Is this actually worth the money?"
- "Will it actually do anything?"
- "Where's the proof?"
- "Can I taste it before I commit?"

### Best Hook
"Your body's natural state is strength and energy. Most drinks take you further away from it. This one doesn't."

### Best Offer
Founder 50 — early access, first batch, locked price. Free quiz to find out how mineral-depleted you are.

## Persona 2 — The Gym Man Done With Fake Pre-Workouts

**Name:** Ryan, 31  
**Location:** Any UK city or suburb  
**Age:** 25–40  
**Lifestyle:** Trains 4–5x per week, follows fitness accounts, has tried multiple pre-workouts, tired of jitters/crash/artificial taste. Interested in ancestral health, carnivore-adjacent ideas, Joe Rogan type content.

- Pre-workout makes his heart race and crashes him
- Doesn't trust the ingredient list on most supplements

- Interested in natural approaches but not "weak" wellness

### Currently Buys

- Electrolyte sachets (LMNT, Precision Hydration)
- Beetroot shots (Beet It)
- Protein powder
- Creatine

### Why They'd Buy Fitrah

- Pomegranate + beet + salt = clean alternative
- Brand feels masculine and no-nonsense
- Beats paying £2.50 for a tiny Beet It shot

### Objections
- "Does it actually work as a pre-workout?"
- "Is it strong enough?"
- "How does it compare to my current pre-workout?"

### Best Hook
"Your pre-workout has 47 ingredients. This has 6. Real food works."

### Best Offer
Pre-workout trial pack — sample box of 6. Comparison guide: "Real food vs fake stimulants."

## Persona 3 — The Blue-Collar Tradesman

**Name:** Lee / Tariq, 34  
**Location:** UK — any region  
**Age:** 28–45  
**Lifestyle:** Builder, electrician, plumber, driver, warehouse — physical work all day. Currently drinks Monster, Red Bull, Lucozade, strong coffee. Eats what's convenient. Cares about energy, not aesthetics.

### Pain Points
- Energy crashes at 2pm
- Drinks things he knows are bad but has no alternative
- Doesn't want complicated — just something that works
- Doesn't trust "health brands" — too soft, too expensive, too complicated
- Body is suffering from hard physical work with poor nutrition
- Wants to feel less destroyed at the end of the day

### Currently Buys
- Energy drinks (Monster, Lucozade, Red Bull)
- Protein bars (occasionally)
- Fast food
- Coffee

### Why They'd Buy Fitrah
- Simple. Real. No nonsense.
- Cheaper than buying 2 energy drinks per day
- If it gives real energy without the crash, he'll become a loyal customer
- Tradesman identity — if JobFilter is for tradesmen, crossover audience exists
- Works at job sites and the drink can be marketed there

### Objections
- "Too expensive for what it is"
- "I don't have time to think about this"
- "Does it actually give me energy?"
- "Sounds like something for gym people, not me"

### Best Hook
"Built for men who actually work. Real energy. Real food. Nothing fake."

### Best Offer
10-pack trial box at a reasonable price. "Replace your energy drink for a week." Simple guarantee.
