## The Concept

A red mineral drink made from real food.  
No synthetic stimulants. No artificial colours. No fake science.  
Pomegranate + beetroot + electrolytes + a few honest additions.  
Made for men who want to hydrate properly, train harder, and feel the difference real food makes.

## 3 Formula Routes

---

### Route 1 — Daily Mineral Hydration (EASIEST MVP)

**Purpose:** Replace synthetic electrolyte drinks. Daily hydration. Easy to make, easy to understand.

**Core ingredients:**
- Pomegranate juice (base — antioxidants, polyphenols, natural colour)
- Beetroot juice (minerals, nitrates, depth of flavour)
- Sea salt (sodium — electrolyte)
- Lemon juice (vitamin C, taste balance, brightness)
- Water (dilution to taste)

**Optional extras:**
- Magnesium (magnesium citrate — easy to add, soluble)
- Potassium (cream of tartar — natural source, cheap)
- Coconut water (extra electrolytes, sweetness, depth)

**Taste profile:** Earthy, tart, slightly sweet, mineral finish. Like a sophisticated fruit juice with bite.

**Use case:** Morning or during training. Replace tap water or synthetic electrolytes. Daily habit drink.

**Cost difficulty:** ★★☆☆☆ (Low — can make at home or small batch)
**Shelf-life difficulty:** ★★★☆☆ (Medium — fresh is short, concentrate is longer)
**Compliance risk:** ★★☆☆☆ (Low — food ingredients, no medical claims)

**Why customers buy:** "I want to hydrate properly without drinking sugar water or chemical drinks."  
**Price estimate:** £2.50–£4 per serving (RTD bottle), £15–£25 for concentrate/powder form

---

### Route 2 — Pre-Workout Red Tonic (HIGH INTEREST, HARDER)

**Purpose:** Replace synthetic pre-workouts. Taken 30–60 minutes before training.

**Core ingredients:**
- Beetroot juice (higher concentration — nitric oxide, blood flow)
- Pomegranate juice (antioxidant, recover faster)
- Ginger (natural warming, anti-inflammatory, taste)
- Sea salt + potassium (electrolyte hit)
- Lemon juice
- Black pepper (absorption aid for nutrients)

**Optional extras:**
- Ashwagandha (stress adaptation — MUST check Novel Food status UK)
- L-citrulline (amino acid — moves this into supplement territory, added complexity)
- Cayenne (warming, circulation)

**Taste profile:** Spicy, earthy, zingy, warming. A proper shot rather than a casual drink.

**Use case:** 30–45 min before training. Shot (60–100ml) or diluted bottle.

**Cost difficulty:** ★★★☆☆ (Medium — ingredient costs manageable, formulation precision matters)
**Shelf-life difficulty:** ★★★★☆ (Hard — fresh ginger, juice — needs preserving or cold-chain)
**Compliance risk:** ★★★☆☆ (Medium — add-ons like ashwagandha need Novel Food check)

**Why customers buy:** "I want a real pre-workout that doesn't make my heart race and doesn't crash me."  
**Price estimate:** £3–£5 per shot, £25–£40 for a box of 10

### Route 3 — Premium Ancestral Tonic (STORY-RICH, HIGHER PRICE)

**Purpose:** A premium, ritual drink. Morning protocol. Status product. High story value.

**Core ingredients:**
- Pomegranate juice (the hero — Qur'anic fruit, antioxidants, colour)
- Beetroot juice

- Sea salt (Himalayan or Celtic — premium mineral story)

**Taste profile:** Complex, earthy, floral, warm, slightly sharp, mineral finish. The "serious" version.

**Use case:** Morning routine, fasted or with food. A daily ritual not a gym drink. The man who has a morning protocol.

**Cost difficulty:** ★★★★☆ (Higher — raw honey, premium salts, ACV)
**Shelf-life difficulty:** ★★★★☆ (Hard — ACV and ginger shorten shelf life)
**Compliance risk:** ★★★☆☆ (Medium — raw honey label requirements, ACV claim risks)

**Why customers buy:** "I want a premium morning ritual drink that is actually good for me. Not a coffee. Not a protein shake. Something real."  
**Price estimate:** £4–£6 per bottle, £35–£60 per case

## MVP Decision — START WITH ROUTE 1

**Winner: Daily Mineral Hydration**

### Why:
- Simplest to make in small batches
- Lowest regulatory risk (pure food ingredients)
- Clearest customer problem (bad hydration, fake electrolyte drinks)
- Widest audience (gymgoers, daily health seekers, Muslim men, tradesmen)
- Can be made fresh at home for testing and sampling
- Can sell as a concentrate or powder version later for better shelf life
- Easiest to validate before manufacturing at scale

### MVP Formula (Exact Starting Point):

**Per 500ml bottle:**
- 150ml pomegranate juice (100% pure, no concentrate where possible)
- 100ml beetroot juice (cold pressed)
- 200ml coconut water (or filtered water for simpler version)
- 50ml lemon juice (fresh squeezed)
- 1/4 tsp sea salt (sodium)
- 1/4 tsp cream of tartar (potassium — optional, add last)
- Small amount magnesium citrate powder (start without — add in V2)
- Optional: 5ml raw honey (sweetness, texture)

**Taste test target:** Earthy, tart, mineral, slight sweetness, not too intense.

## Raw Milk Position

**Do not use raw milk as a commercial product yet.**

- UK raw milk sales are heavily restricted — direct from farm only
- Liability and food safety risk is very high for a new brand
- Use raw milk as content/philosophy — "why I drink raw milk" — not as a product

Position: "We believe in real food. We talk about raw milk, tallow, eggs, beef and real dairy. Our drink uses only food ingredients we can legally and safely sell."
