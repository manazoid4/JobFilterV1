## Top 10 Shortlist

### 1. Fitrah
- **Meaning:** Innate nature — the Islamic concept of natural, uncorrupted human state
- **Vibe:** Deep, grounded, faith-compatible without being preachy, unique
- **Why it works:** Speaks directly to the audience without alienating others. "Return to your Fitrah" works as both a product message and a life message. Non-Muslim customers won't know the religious meaning — they'll just feel the word sounds natural and strong.
- **Risk:** Can be mispronounced by non-Muslims. Some might Google it and find it religious — but for this audience, that's a feature.
- **Tagline:** *Return to your nature.*

### 2. Asil
- **Meaning:** Original, noble, of pure origin (Arabic)
- **Vibe:** Premium, clean, masculine, minimal
- **Why it works:** Short, punchy, easy to say, looks great on a bottle. "Asil — made from original sources." Premium without fake luxury.
- **Risk:** Some might mispronounce (AH-sil). Not clearly health/drink-related without context.
- **Tagline:** *From original sources.*

### 3. Rumman
- **Meaning:** Pomegranate (Arabic — mentioned in the Qur'an as a fruit of paradise)
- **Vibe:** Story-rich, product-direct, culturally meaningful, unique
- **Why it works:** First product is a pomegranate tonic. Name tells you exactly what it is. Meaningful. Memorable. Defensible. Grows with the brand.
- **Risk:** Non-Arab speakers may not know the connection. Might be hard to say for some.
- **Tagline:** *The original red.*

### 4. Khalis
- **Meaning:** Pure, genuine, unadulterated (Arabic)
- **Vibe:** Honest, clean, simple, confident
- **Why it works:** "Khalis" does the work that "pure" or "clean" does but feels different, less generic. Works across products. Simple to say.
- **Risk:** Risk of confusion with "Khalid" or similar names. Less brandable on its own without story context.
- **Tagline:** *Nothing added. Nothing removed.*

### 5. Forge
- **Meaning:** Where metal is made under heat and pressure — strength through craft
- **Vibe:** Hard, masculine, craft-focused, process-driven
- **Why it works:** Works for a gym audience and tradesman audience equally. The idea of "forging" a better body, a better routine, a better version of yourself. No religious connotation — purely universal.
- **Risk:** Many brands called "Forge." Check trademark before committing. Less unique.
- **Tagline:** *Made under pressure.*

### 6. Marrow
- **Meaning:** Bone marrow — the deepest, richest nourishment. Also "the essence of something."
- **Vibe:** Deep, real, ancestral, slightly dark, very premium
- **Why it works:** "The marrow of real nutrition." Unusual name for a drink brand — memorable. Signals ancestral food philosophy without being preachy.
- **Risk:** Bone connotation might confuse people for a drink. Strong — might need explanation.
- **Tagline:** *Deep nourishment. Real results.*

### 7. Quwwah
- **Meaning:** Strength, power (Arabic)
- **Vibe:** Strong, masculine, faith-compatible
- **Why it works:** Directly states the outcome. Sounds powerful when said correctly. Q-brands are rare and distinctive.
- **Risk:** Hard for most UK customers to pronounce or spell. "Koo-wah" needs phonetic help. High friction for search/word-of-mouth.
- **Tagline:** *Your strength. Your nature.*

### 8. Flint
- **Meaning:** Hard stone used to make fire — ancient, essential, reliable
- **Vibe:** Hard, old, minimal, slightly rugged, British
- **Why it works:** Short, punchy, very English, universally understood. "Flint" feels like a proper brand name. Ancient + practical.
- **Risk:** No cultural specificity. Doesn't speak to the Muslim/ancestral audience as directly. Some Flint brands exist.
- **Tagline:** *Spark something.*

### 9. Root Oath
- **Meaning:** A commitment to going back to natural foundations
- **Vibe:** Grounded, principled, lifestyle-forward
- **Why it works:** Two-word brand names are memorable. "Root" = ancestral, natural. "Oath" = discipline, commitment. Together = lifestyle brand with a promise.
- **Risk:** Two words = harder on packaging/logo. Slightly wordy.
- **Tagline:** *The promise of real food.*

### 10. Safi
- **Meaning:** Pure, clear, unfiltered (Arabic)
- **Vibe:** Clean, fresh, simple, accessible
- **Why it works:** Very easy to say and spell. Short. Works in English without explanation — "safi" sounds like "safe" and "satisfy." Known name in the Arab world. Clean brand potential.
- **Risk:** Common enough as a personal name that it could feel like someone's name, not a brand. Less muscular than the product needs.
- **Tagline:** *Pure by nature.*

## Best 3 Final Recommendations

### #1 — FITRAH ⭐ (Recommended)
The deepest, most meaningful, most defensible name.
- Speaks to the core audience at a philosophical level
- Non-Muslims will read "natural state" energy without knowing the religious meaning
- "Return to your Fitrah" is a brand concept, not just a name
- Unique — very few brands use it
- Works across drinks, digital products, community, lifestyle

**Brand:** Fitrah. *Return to your nature.*

---

### #2 — RUMMAN
If you want the name to be product-forward and story-rich.
- Pomegranate is the hero ingredient
- Qur'anic fruit — faith angle without preaching
- Memorable, unusual, differentiated
- Grows well as the first product name or as the drink sub-brand under a bigger name

**Brand:** Rumman. *The original red.*

---

### #3 — ASIL
If you want premium, clean and cross-cultural.
- "Of original stock" works for ancestral food philosophy
- Short, premium, minimal on packaging
- Works if the brand grows beyond just Muslim audience
- Less polarising, more universally premium

**Brand:** Asil. *From original sources.*

---

## Recommendation

Go with **FITRAH** as the brand name.  
Use **RUMMAN** as the product name for the Red Mineral Tonic.

So: **Fitrah | Rumman Red Tonic** — the first product from Fitrah.

This gives you a brand that can grow, and a product name that tells the story.
