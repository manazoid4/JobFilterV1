# Competitor Research - 2 May 2026

## Main Position

JobFilter should not copy job boards.

The gap:

Trades already have tools for quotes, finance, websites, materials, reviews, and admin.

They still do not have a trusted system that says:

Here are the few jobs near you worth chasing this week.

## OneSite

Source: https://www.one.site/

What to emulate:

- Clear construction job-to-be-done.
- Compliance-grade proof.
- Simple operational language.

JobFilter version:

- Lead proof card: source, date found, planning ref, confidence, contact signal.
- "Before arrival" logic: leads arrive before everyone else sees them.

Avoid:

- Enterprise H&S feel.

## PriceBuilder

Sources:

- https://www.pricebuilder.co.uk/pricing
- https://www.pricebuilder.co.uk/faq

What to emulate:

- Low-risk free taste.
- Simple pricing.
- Quote-ready workflow later.

JobFilter version:

- Free preview shows signal.
- Paid unlocks source proof, contact signal, deadline, exact value, and WhatsApp action.

Avoid:

- Becoming a quoting tool before lead quality is proven.

## Time To Scale UK

Source: https://timetoscale.co.uk/

What to emulate:

- Outcome-first copy.
- Missed-call urgency.
- Clear ROI framing.

JobFilter version:

- REAL LEADS.
- NO CHASING.
- NO COMPETING.
- STAY IN CONTROL.

Avoid:

- Website/SEO agency territory.

## GoTrade Flow

Status:

Could not verify a credible UK trades/construction product from current research.

Action:

Do not benchmark until a URL is provided.

Useful concept:

Lead flow:

`New -> Worth Calling -> Contacted -> Quoted -> Won/Lost`

Keep it WhatsApp-first, not CRM-heavy.

## Invoice Ian

Source: https://invoiceian.com/

What to emulate:

- Short check.
- Cash-flow pain copy.
- No-obligation language.

JobFilter version:

- Later flag for cash-heavy jobs: deposit needed before materials.

Avoid:

- Financing inside JobFilter before lead quality and paid conversion work.

## News Feed Rule

Do not build a blog.

Build:

This week's signals that affect your work.

Use:

- Planning approvals near you.
- Material price movement.
- Local contractor wins.
- HSE enforcement trends.
- Skills shortages by trade.
- Regulation changes that affect quoting or site work.
