# Unfair Advantage Stack

Single signals are easy to copy.

Signal fusion is the moat.

## Core Stack

### Homeowner Retrofit Lead

- planning application submitted or pending
- EPC F/G or weak D/E
- affluent postcode
- detached/semi-detached property
- high floor area
- recent sale or probate/auction indicator
- solar/roof orientation or heat-pump suitability

Likely work:

- insulation
- heat pump
- glazing
- roofing
- solar
- extension

### Active Site Lead

- planning/building control event
- scaffold permit
- skip permit
- road occupation or streetworks nearby
- fresh activity date

Likely work:

- roofing
- rendering
- external repairs
- extension works
- solar
- chimney work

### Commercial Fit-Out Lead

- new company registration or branch opening
- licensing application
- change of use
- retail/restaurant/clinic/Ofsted registration
- commercial property movement

Likely work:

- electrical
- HVAC
- fire safety
- flooring
- signage
- partitions
- plumbing

### Distressed Property Lead

- probate notice
- auction listing
- vacant-home signal
- low EPC
- recent price-paid movement
- storm/flood/subsidence cluster

Likely work:

- clearance
- refurbishment
- roofing
- damp
- structural repair
- resale prep

## Why Competitors Struggle To Copy

- Marketplaces wait for homeowners to submit jobs.
- SEO/PPC only sees search demand.
- Planning-list vendors often sell raw applications.
- Enterprise intelligence tools are too broad and expensive for individual trades.
- Signal fusion needs connector coverage, scoring, feedback, and local delivery.

## JobFilter Wedge

JobFilter should sell:

> We find the early money signals, filter the weak ones out, and route the best jobs to the right trade before the work becomes noisy.

## Implementation Rule

Every Gold lead should eventually explain its stack:

- why now
- why this trade
- why this patch
- why this value
- why this confidence

Keep raw source detail internal where it creates competitive risk.
