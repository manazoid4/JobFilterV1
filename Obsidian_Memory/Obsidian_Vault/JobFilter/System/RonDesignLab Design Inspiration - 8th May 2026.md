## Not a Competitor — A Design Inspiration Source

RonDesignLab does NOT compete with JobFilter. They design products for enterprises and startups. But their design patterns, visual language, and UX structure are worth studying for JobFilter's brutalist yellow aesthetic.

---

## Design Patterns Worth Stealing

### 1. Sticky-Scroll Side-by-Side Layout
- Left side: sticky content panel that updates as you scroll
- Right side: scrolling visual cards
- **Apply to JobFilter**: Use for the "5 signals" section. Left side explains each signal, right side shows live example cards.

### 2. "Are they the ones who can help me?" Section Framing
- RonDesignLab uses conversational questions as section headers
- "Are they skilled enough?" → case study proof
- "Are they trustworthy?" → testimonials
- "Who are they?" → team section
- **Apply to JobFilter**: Use conversational headers: "Does this actually work?" → lead proof. "Who built this?" → founder story. "What does it cost?" → pricing.

### 3. Sprint-Based Timeline Display
- "From 2 sprints", "From 6 sprints" next to each service
- Sets clear time expectations upfront
- **Apply to JobFilter**: Show time-to-value: "First lead in minutes", "Full scan in 30 seconds", "Setup in 2 minutes"

### 4. Segmented Audience Entry
- "How do you define yourself?" → Startups / Dreamers / SMB / Enterprise
- Each segment gets tailored messaging and services
- **Apply to JobFilter**: "What trade are you?" → Plumber / Roofer / Electrician / Builder / Landscaper. Each gets trade-specific lead examples.

### 5. Metric-Heavy Case Studies
- "+59% increase in users", "+72% satisfaction", "+37% revenue"
- Specific numbers, not vague claims
- **Apply to JobFilter**: "£4,200 job won", "3 minutes to first alert", "0 shared leads"

### 6. "Our SEO asked to add this here" Self-Aware Humor
- RonDesignLab adds a cheeky section acknowledging their own SEO
- Shows personality and doesn't take itself too seriously
- **Apply to JobFilter**: Add personality. "Built in Birmingham, not a corporate lab." "No investors. No board meetings. Just a system that works."

### 7. Vertical Text + Bold Typography
- RonDesignLab uses vertical text for section headers: "Are / they / skilled / enough?"
- Creates visual rhythm and breaks monotony
- **Apply to JobFilter**: Use bold, oversized typography for key claims. "REAL / LEADS. / NO / CHASING."

### 8. Sticky CTA Footer
- "Let's talk" section always accessible
- Multiple contact methods (email, Instagram, Dribbble, Behance)
- **Apply to JobFilter**: Sticky WhatsApp button on every page. Always one tap away.

### 9. Persona-Based Testimonials
- Each testimonial includes: name, role, company, specific quote
- Not anonymous "great service!" — specific and credible
- **Apply to JobFilter**: "Dave, Roofer, Birmingham — Won a £3,800 re-roofing job from a planning alert. First week."

### 10. Process Transparency
- Brief → Proposal → Contract shown as visual steps
- Each step has sub-items explaining what happens
- **Apply to JobFilter**: "Sign up → Connect WhatsApp → Get leads" — 3 steps, visual, no mystery.

## Visual Language for JobFilter (Brutalist Yellow)

From RonDesignLab's approach, adapt these principles:

| RonDesignLab | JobFilter Adaptation |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Clean white backgrounds | Black background, yellow accents |
| Rounded cards, soft shadows | Sharp edges, no shadows, high contrast |
| Corporate photography | No stock photos. Real screenshots, real data |
| Emoji section markers | No emojis. Bold text, borders, numbers |
| Pastel accent colors | DeWalt yellow (#FFD100), black, white only |
| Smiling team photos | Founder photo, straight-faced, working |
| "Let's talk" friendly CTAs | "ENTER THE INTAKE" / "CONTROL THE JOBS" |

## What NOT to Copy

1. **Long forms** — RonDesignLab has a 7-field contact form. JobFilter should never ask for more than name + trade + WhatsApp number.
2. **Corporate language** — "Digital experience agency," "user-centric design methodology." JobFilter speaks trades, not tech.
3. **Sprint-based timelines** — "From 6 sprints" means weeks. JobFilter delivers in minutes. Don't slow the perception down.
4. **Enterprise focus** — RonDesignLab targets Samsung and Ford. JobFilter targets individual tradesmen. Keep it personal, not corporate.
5. **Emoji overload** — RonDesignLab uses 🚀✨💼🎨 throughout. JobFilter uses zero emojis in product copy.
