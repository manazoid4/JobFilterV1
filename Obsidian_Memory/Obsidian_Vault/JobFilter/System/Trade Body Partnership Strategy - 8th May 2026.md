## PARTNERSHIP MODEL RECOMMENDATION

### The "Member Benefit" Model (Recommended for all targets)

Don't pitch JobFilter as a product to sell. Pitch it as a **member benefit** that makes the trade body look good.

**Structure:**
1. **Exclusive member rate:** £19/mo for members of partnered body (vs £29 public Founding rate). Body gets to advertise this as a perk of membership.
2. **Revenue share:** 15-25% of subscription revenue from members who sign up through the partnership. Body gets passive income.
3. **Co-branded landing page:** `/napit`, `/niceic`, `/eca` — "JobFilter for [Body] Members." Shows body logo, explains the benefit, tracks referrals.
4. **Quarterly member webinar:** "How to find jobs before they hit the directories." Delivered by JobFilter, hosted by the body. Positions body as forward-thinking.
5. **Newsletter feature:** One email per quarter to the body's membership. "New member benefit: find planning-approved jobs in your area."

**Why this works:**
- Trade bodies need to justify membership fees. A tangible benefit that saves members money and wins them jobs does that.
- Revenue share creates ongoing incentive for the body to promote JobFilter.
- Co-branded page builds trust — members see the body's endorsement, not a random ad.
- Webinar provides content the body would otherwise have to create themselves.

**What to avoid:**
- Don't pitch as "advertising" or "sponsorship." Bodies hate being seen as selling their members' attention.
- Don't ask for email list access upfront. That's what Buildscout got from FMB and it's the hardest ask. Start with directory placement + co-branded page, then negotiate email access after proving value.
- Don't compete with the body's own directory. Position JobFilter as complementary: "Your directory helps homeowners find you. JobFilter helps you find jobs before homeowners even know they need you."
