### 2. What Competitors Are Doing for SEO

**Checkatrade (checkatrade.com):**
- 2.86M organic visits/month (March 2026)
- Traffic is overwhelmingly branded — "checkatrade", "check a trade" = 90%+ of visits
- Organic unbranded reach is via local city/trade directory pages (e.g., "roofers Birmingham") — takes years and high domain authority to compete with
- Blog covers: cost guides ("loft conversion cost 2026"), "how to find a tradesman" content
- Content strategy: cost guides for homeowners — not for tradesmen

**MyBuilder (mybuilder.com):**
- Top result for "loft conversion cost" and related buyer-intent guides
- No content targeting tradesman-side queries ("how to get more building work")

**BuildAlert (buildalert.uk) — DIRECT COMPETITOR NOT IN VAULT:**
- Planning-data lead platform. Uses a hub-and-spoke SEO model: one long-form pillar page per topic + city-specific sub-pages
- Ranks for: "construction leads [city]" (Manchester, London, Bristol, Birmingham, Kent, Surrey, Essex)
- Targets: "builder leads", "loft conversion leads", "extension leads", "planning application leads"
- No EPC angle. No council contract angle. JobFilter has two data sources BuildAlert does not touch.
- Pay-per-use at £2/letter — prints and posts a letter to the homeowner on the tradesman's behalf. No WhatsApp. No scoring.
- Covers 400+ councils, ~20,000 planning applications/week
- **BuildAlert's SEO is JobFilter's content blueprint.** They have proven the keyword cluster works. JobFilter should build an identical structure but for all three data sources (planning + EPC + council contracts) and with better content.

**Buildscout (buildscout.co.uk):**
- Similar planning data angle, more commercial/enterprise slant
- Targets "top UK construction lead generation sources 2026" style content
- No WhatsApp, no scoring engine, higher price point

**planning.org.uk:**
- Covers 241 UK planning authorities daily
- SEO targets: "planning alerts UK", "planning application alerts"
- Purely informational/alert service — no scoring, no trades focus

### 4. Keyword Data: What Ranks and What Converts

**Cost guide keywords — high volume, homeowner-side (Checkatrade/MyBuilder own these):**
- "how much does a loft conversion cost" — extremely competitive, Checkatrade/Which?/MyBuilder dominate
- "how much does a boiler replacement cost" — MyJobQuote ranks #1
- "house rewire cost UK 2026" — multiple directories compete

Do NOT try to rank for these. Checkatrade has years of domain authority. These are homeowner-intent keywords — wrong audience for JobFilter.

**Tradesman-intent keywords — low competition, JobFilter opportunity:**
- "construction leads near me" — low competition
- "planning application leads for builders" — near-zero. BuildAlert is the main ranker with thin content
- "EPC leads for installers" — near-zero. No dedicated result exists
- "how to find building work without Checkatrade" — near-zero. Huge conversion potential
- "council tender leads tradespeople" — near-zero. Complex topic with no simple tool answering it

**Local keyword pattern — copy BuildAlert's model:**
BuildAlert ranks for "construction leads [city]" across 12+ UK cities with simple 300-500 word pages. JobFilter can outrank them with better content + better data from three sources. A 1,000-word page per city with real planning and EPC data for that area would outrank them within 3-4 months.
