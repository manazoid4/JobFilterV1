## Strengths

1. **WhatsApp-first sales** — Zero friction. Tradesmen already live on WhatsApp. No forms, no calls, no booking links. Just "message me."
2. **Outcome-focused copy** — "If you're not in the top 3 on Google, your customers are calling someone else." Direct, painful, true.
3. **Comparison table** — Side-by-side vs agencies (£2,500+ for 5-10 pages vs £499 for 200+ pages). Makes the decision obvious.
4. **Scarcity tactic** — "Currently booking May/June 2026 — limited spots." Creates urgency without being sleazy.
5. **Case studies with real data** — Shows exact keywords, positions, days to rank. Not vague "increased traffic" claims.
6. **Two-tier pricing** — £499 captures one-time buyers. £200/mo captures recurring revenue. Smart.
7. **No jargon** — Speaks like a tradesman would. No "digital transformation" or "SEO optimisation strategy."
8. **Video proof** — "Watch: Why 99% of Local Business Websites Don't Work" — recorded live, no script. Builds trust.

## Weaknesses

1. **No lead generation** — He builds websites that rank, but doesn't qualify or deliver leads. Tradesmen still have to wait for the phone to ring.
2. **No multi-signal intelligence** — Purely SEO/planning. No EPC, no contracts, no Companies House, no Land Registry.
3. **Slow results** — 2-4 weeks for rankings, 3-6 months for meaningful SEO. JobFilter delivers leads within minutes.
4. **No WhatsApp delivery** — Ironically, he sells via WhatsApp but doesn't deliver leads via WhatsApp.
5. **Single-signal** — Only Google search visibility. Doesn't help trades find jobs before competitors even know they exist.
6. **No scoring/qualification** — A website brings enquiries, but doesn't tell you which ones are worth chasing.
7. **Small portfolio** — 25+ sites is not a lot of proof. Some case studies show "—" for live rankings.

## What JobFilter Can Learn From Dean Keating

1. **WhatsApp is the sales channel, not just delivery** — Dean's entire sales process is WhatsApp. JobFilter should add a "Message us on WhatsApp" CTA alongside the product. Tradesmen prefer it to email or forms.
2. **Comparison tables win** — Dean's "£2,500 agency vs £499 me" table is devastating. JobFilter needs a "Checkatrade vs JobFilter" table on the pricing page.
3. **Scarcity without sleaze** — "Currently booking May/June" works because it's true (he's one person). JobFilter can use "Founding member pricing — locks in before public launch."
4. **Case studies with numbers** — Dean shows "#2 ranking in 7 days" not "great results." JobFilter should show "Gold lead delivered in 3 minutes, £4,200 job won."
5. **Two-tier model** — One-time + recurring. JobFilter could offer a "one-time scan report" (£29) alongside the subscription.
6. **"This is not for everyone" section** — Dean's qualification copy filters out bad-fit clients. JobFilter should add: "This isn't for trades who want 50 shared leads. It's for trades who want 5 real ones."
7. **90-day guarantee framing** — Dean guarantees results or keeps working free. JobFilter should guarantee: "If you don't get a gold lead in 30 days, we extend free."

## What JobFilter Does Better

1. **Proactive, not reactive** — Dean's sites wait for customers to search. JobFilter finds jobs before the homeowner even knows they need a tradesman.
2. **Multi-signal intelligence** — 5 data sources vs 1 (Google). This is the structural advantage.
3. **Speed** — Minutes vs weeks. A plumber can't wait 3 weeks for a website to rank when there's a job available today.
4. **Scoring** — GOLD/SILVER/BIN tells trades which jobs to chase. Dean's websites bring all enquiries equally.
5. **Exclusivity** — No shared leads. Dean's websites are public — anyone can visit them.
6. **Price** — £29/mo vs £499 or £200/mo. JobFilter is accessible to every tradesman.
7. **Control** — JobFilter gives trades control over what work comes in. A website is passive.
