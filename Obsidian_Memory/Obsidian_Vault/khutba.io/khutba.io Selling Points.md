## Core Pitch
> "Every Muslim in your masjid understands the khutba — in their language, live, on screen."

## Top 7 Points
1. **Screen-first display** — On the screen everyone already looks at. Not on personal phones.
2. **Unlimited minutes** — Every Friday. Every Ramadan. No caps. MinbarLive caps at 100 min.
3. **Full inclusion** — Urdu, Somali, Bengali, English speakers all follow along
4. **Zero hardware cost** — any existing screen + laptop, open browser, done
5. **Under 10-min setup** — account → mic → languages → live
6. **Priced for masjids** — £29/mo. Less than MinbarLive's €59/mo entry.
7. **Archive = institutional memory** — every khutba stored and searchable

## Objections
| Objection               | Answer                                    |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Imam already translates | Free the imam — let him focus on delivery |
| Too expensive           | Less than a box of flyers per month (£29) |
| Tech unreliable         | Offline fallback, local network support   |
| Arabic displays wrong   | RTL built in from day one                 |
| GDPR concerns           | Opt-in archive, UK data storage           |
| Why not MinbarLive?     | They cap minutes. We don't. They're phone-only. We're screen-first. |

## Target Customers
- Masjid committees (decision makers)
- Islamic centres with multi-ethnic congregations
- Friday Jumu'ah — largest weekly gathering
- Ramadan tarawih — high attendance
- Nikah / Janazah — formal speeches

## Distribution
- **Primary:** WhatsApp (masjid committee groups)
- **Secondary:** In-person visits after Jumu'ah
- **Tertiary:** Facebook/Instagram (UK Muslim demographics)
- **Referral:** "Refer another masjid, get 1 month free"
