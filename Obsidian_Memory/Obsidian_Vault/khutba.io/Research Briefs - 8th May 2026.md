# Research Briefs — 8th May 2026

> Short-form research briefs on market opportunities, threats, and strategic recommendations for khutba.io.

---

## Brief 1: The Screen-First Moat

**Problem:** Every live translation competitor (MinbarLive, church tools) is phone-first. Congregation members scan a QR code and read on their phones.

**Reality:** Masjids already have screens. They use them for prayer times, announcements, and Quran verses. The screen is the focal point of the room.

**Opportunity:** Build the BEST screen display experience. Large text, auto-scroll, RTL support, masjid branding, multi-language side-by-side. Make it so good that masjids prefer it over phone-based solutions.

**Why it works:**
- Older congregation members struggle with phone screens
- Not everyone has a smartphone
- Screen creates a shared experience (everyone follows together)
- Masjid committees want visible proof their investment is working
- Screens are already there — zero hardware cost

**Risk:** MinbarLive could add a screen mode. Speed is critical.

**Action:** Ship screen display in Phase 1. Make it the hero feature.

## Brief 2: The UK Beachhead Strategy

**Problem:** Going global from day one dilutes focus and marketing spend.

**Reality:** The UK has ~2,000 masjids, concentrated in specific cities. Birmingham alone has 150+ masjids. The South Asian community (Urdu, Bengali, Somali speakers) is the largest non-Arabic-speaking Muslim demographic in the UK.

**Opportunity:** Dominate Birmingham first. Get 10-20 masjids in Birmingham using khutba.io. Use word-of-mouth to spread to Manchester, London, Leeds, Bradford.

**Why Birmingham:**
- Large South Asian Muslim population
- Multi-ethnic congregations = high need for translation
- Local trust matters — "built in Birmingham" is a real advantage
- Lower competition than London
- Easier to do in-person demos and support

**Go-to-market:**
1. Identify 20 target masjids in Birmingham
2. Offer free 3-month pilot to first 5
3. Collect testimonials and case studies
4. Use testimonials to sell to remaining 15
5. Expand to Manchester/Leeds/Bradford (similar demographics)
6. National UK rollout
7. Then Europe (starting with UK diaspora communities)

**Timeline:** 6 months to dominate Birmingham, 12 months for UK national.

## Brief 3: The Ramadan Play

**Problem:** Masjids buy software irregularly. There's no natural buying cycle.

**Reality:** Ramadan is the ONE time every masjid committee is thinking about improving the congregation experience. Attendance goes up 3-5x. Non-Arabic speakers attend more. The need for translation is most visible.

**Opportunity:** Make khutba.io THE Ramadan preparation tool. Launch 2-3 months before Ramadan. Market it as "Get your masjid Ramadan-ready."

**Ramadan-specific features:**
- Taraweeh mode (longer sessions, different scrolling speed)
- Ramadan branding/theme option
- "Ramadan Ready" badge for masjids using khutba.io
- Pre-Ramadan setup checklist
- Post-Ramadan analytics ("Your khutbahs were viewed in X languages")

**Pricing play:**
- Annual billing: "Pay for 10 months, get Ramadan free"
- Ramadan-only plan: £49 for 2 months (Ramadan + Shawwal)
- Free trial during Rajab (the month before Ramadan)

**Marketing:**
- Start outreach in Rajab (2 months before Ramadan)
- "Is your masjid ready for Ramadan?" email campaign
- Target masjid committees on WhatsApp (where they communicate)
- Partner with local Islamic scholars for endorsements

**Revenue impact:** Ramadan acquisition could drive 30-40% of annual signups.

## Brief 4: The Unlimited Minutes Advantage

**Problem:** MinbarLive caps minutes at every tier. A 100-min Standard plan covers ~2.5 khutbas/week. Ramadan taraweeh (1-1.5 hours/night for 30 nights) blows through this in one week.

**Reality:** khutba.io's API cost is ~$3.26/mo per masjid at 2 khutbas/week. Even at 4 khutbas/week + Ramadan, the cost is under $10/mo. The margin at £29/mo is 85%+.

**Opportunity:** Offer UNLIMITED minutes at every tier. This is a massive competitive advantage that MinbarLive cannot easily match (their pricing model is built around minute caps).

**Messaging:**
- "Unlimited khutbahs. Every Friday. Every Ramadan. No surprises."
- "MinbarLive charges you for minutes. We charge you for peace of mind."
- "Your khutbah isn't a metered service."

**Risk:** A small number of masjids might run very long sessions (5+ hours/day). Even then, the API cost is manageable. Set a fair use policy at 20 hours/month (covers even the most active masjid).

**Action:** Make "unlimited" a core selling point on the pricing page.

## Brief 5: The Imam-Facing Opportunity (Phase 4+)

**Problem:** MinbarLive has built AI KhutbaAssistant — an AI tool that helps imams prepare khutbahs. This is a feature that directly targets the imam (the content creator), not just the congregation (the consumer).

**Reality:** Imams are the gatekeepers. If an imam loves a tool, the masjid committee will buy it. MinbarLive understands this.

**Opportunity:** Build a lightweight imam-facing tool as a Phase 4 feature. Not a full AI assistant — start simpler.

**Phase 4a (simple):**
- Khutbah script upload → auto-generate translation preview
- Imam can review translations before going live
- Build trust in translation quality

**Phase 4b (advanced):**
- AI Khutbah outline generator (topic → structure → key points)
- Quran verse lookup and translation
- Hadith reference suggestions
- Previous khutbah archive search

**Why not build it now:**
- Phase 1-3 should focus on the core product (live translation)
- Imam tools are a nice-to-have, not a must-have
- MinbarLive's AI assistant is still early — watch how it's received first

**Positioning:** "Built for imams, loved by congregations"

## Brief 6: The WhatsApp Distribution Channel

**Problem:** Masjid committees don't read emails. They don't browse Product Hunt. They communicate on WhatsApp.

**Reality:** Every masjid committee has a WhatsApp group. Decisions are made there. Recommendations spread there.

**Opportunity:** Build a WhatsApp-first distribution strategy.

**Tactics:**
1. Create shareable WhatsApp messages (not emails)
2. Build a WhatsApp bot for masjid admins to try khutba.io
3. Encourage existing users to share in their committee WhatsApp groups
4. Create short demo videos optimized for WhatsApp sharing (vertical, <60 seconds)
5. Offer referral discounts ("Refer another masjid, get 1 month free")

**WhatsApp message template:**
> "Assalamu Alaikum. We started using khutba.io at [Masjid Name] — it shows live translations of the khutbah on our screen in Urdu, Bengali, and English. Takes 10 minutes to set up. Costs less than a box of flyers per month. Want me to show you? [link]"

**Why it works:**
- Personal recommendation from another masjid committee
- Low-cost framing ("less than a box of flyers")
- Specific languages mentioned (relevant to UK masjids)
- Social proof (another masjid is already using it)

## Brief 7: The Digital Signage Integration Play

**Problem:** Many masjids already use digital signage software (ScreenCloud, Yodeck, custom solutions) for prayer times and announcements.

**Reality:** These tools manage screens but don't do live translation. khutba.io does live translation but doesn't manage other screen content.

**Opportunity:** Integrate khutba.io AS A WIDGET into existing digital signage platforms. Instead of competing for the screen, become the content that runs on it.

**How it works:**
- khutba.io provides a URL endpoint that displays live translations
- Digital signage software adds this URL as a "web page" widget
- Masjid runs prayer times + khutba.io translations on the same screen
- No need to replace existing signage software

**Benefits:**
- Removes adoption friction (no need to replace current system)
- Positions khutba.io as complementary, not competitive
- Opens up masjids that are locked into existing signage contracts
- Could lead to official partnerships with signage companies

**Action:** Build a "kiosk mode" URL that works as a widget. Document integration guides for major signage platforms.

## Brief 8: The Archive as Institutional Memory

**Problem:** Masjids lose khutbah content every week. No searchable record. No way to revisit important topics.

**Reality:** Khutbahs contain valuable Islamic knowledge. Communities want to reference past khutbahs. New imams want to know what was previously covered.

**Opportunity:** Build a searchable khutbah archive as a Phase 3 feature. This becomes a stickiness feature — once a masjid has 6 months of archive, they won't cancel.

**Archive features:**
- Search by topic, date, imam, language
- Full-text search of translations
- Export as PDF or text
- Share individual khutbahs via link
- Analytics (most viewed khutbahs, language usage)

**Monetization:**
- Archive included in £59+ tiers
- £10/mo add-on for £29 tier
- Public archive page (masjid can share with community)

**Long-term play:** Aggregate anonymized khutbah data → "Most common khutbah topics in UK masjids" → thought leadership content → media coverage → brand awareness.

## Brief 10: What khutba.io Should NOT Build

It's as important to know what NOT to build as what to build.

### Don't Build (yet):
1. **AI Khutbah Assistant** — MinbarLive has it. Let them test the market first.
2. **Podcast/Studio Mode** — Too niche. Most masjids don't need it.
3. **Meeting Translation** — Different use case, different buyer.
4. **Donation Processing** — Regulatory nightmare. Not core.
5. **Prayer Time Calculation** — Commodity feature. Many free tools exist.
6. **Mobile App** — Browser-based is fine. No one wants another app.
7. **Community Social Features** — Not the problem we're solving.
8. **Video Streaming** — Bandwidth-heavy, not core value.

### Build Only When:
- A feature is requested by 5+ paying masjids
- A competitor's version of the feature is getting traction
- It directly supports the core value proposition (live translation on screen)
