# Readability Research — NN/g + Baymard + CRO Benchmarks (2024–2026)

Companion to: [[Readability + Attention Span Conversion Brief]]

## Key updates to the brief

### 1. Four scanning patterns, not three — and layer-cake wins

NN/g (2024) now identifies **four** scanning patterns: F-pattern, spotted, layer-cake, and **commitment**.

- **F-pattern is now a failure mode** — it emerges when content lacks structure. Don't design *for* it; design *against* it with strong headings.
- **Layer-cake is "by far the most effective"** pattern to design for. Heading-driven scanning is what high-converting pages optimise for.

Source: [NN/g — Text Scanning Patterns: Eyetracking Evidence](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/text-scanning-patterns-eyetracking/), [NN/g — Layer-Cake Pattern](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/layer-cake-pattern-scanning/)

### 2. The "8 seconds" stat is dead

The Microsoft 2015 figure has been debunked. University of Chicago longitudinal data shows attention spans haven't shortened — users are **selectively impatient**.

- First impressions form in **~0.05s** (Google/Lindgaard).
- Stay-or-leave decisions are made within **5–7s**.
- Reframe: you have **one glance to signal relevance**.

Source: Room4Media review; ContentGrip on selective attention.

### 3. CTA copy — verb + outcome, 3–5 words

- Single-word CTAs ("Submit", "Send") **underperform by ~60%**.
- AdEvolver's 90 highest-converting CTAs average **3.4 words**, verb-led, naming the outcome.
- HubSpot's 330k-CTA study: **personalised/contextual CTAs convert 202% better** than generic ones.

Apply: `Get this week's leads` ≫ `Sign up`. Trade-specific (`Get plumbing jobs near me`) beats generic every time.

Source: [Foundry CRO 2026](https://foundrycro.com/blog/cta-button-conversion-rate-benchmarks-2026/), [VWO](https://vwo.com/blog/high-converting-call-to-action-button-examples/)

### 4. Hero — ≤ 8 words, problem-first

- Krug's rule: "remove half, then half again."
- Heroes that name the **problem before the solution** outperform feature-led heroes (Splitbase, Omniconvert).
- Recognisable testimonial/video inside hero lifted B2B conversion **~34%**.

Avoid feature words. Trades convert on outcome words: "job", "paid", "this week".

Source: [Splitbase](https://splitbase.com/blog/hero-section), [Omniconvert](https://www.omniconvert.com/blog/hero-section-examples/)

### 5. Above-the-fold attention

- NN/g latest: **57% of viewing time spent above the fold; 74% within first two screenfuls.**
- Mobile attention drops faster than desktop (smaller viewport).
- **Mobile hero must self-contain the value prop in one viewport.**

Mobile above-fold checklist: headline + one-line sub + CTA + one piece of proof.

Source: [NN/g — Scrolling and Attention](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/scrolling-and-attention/)

### 6. Form length

- Forms ≤ 5 fields convert **~120% better** than longer forms (Baymard).
- 22% of users abandon flows perceived as too long (Unbounce).
- Trust seals adjacent to CTA measurably raise perceived safety.

For JobFilter signup: trade, postcode, WhatsApp number, plan, pay. Nothing else.

Source: [Baymard B2B UX](https://baymard.com/research/business-to-business), [Unbounce B2B CRO 2025](https://unbounce.com/conversion-rate-optimization/b2b-conversion-rates/)

## 5 Implementation Rules for JobFilter (research-backed)

### Rule 1 — Design for layer-cake scanning

Every section needs a benefit-bearing **subheading a tradesman can read alone**. If you stripped all the body copy, the subheadings alone should sell the product.

Bad: `How it works` → Good: `Real homeowner jobs, sent to your WhatsApp before competitors spot them.`

### Rule 2 — Hero headline ≤ 8 words, problem-first, one specific proof number

Format: `[Problem]. [Specific outcome].`
Example: `Stop chasing dead leads. One paying job a week.`

Sub-line names the mechanism in one breath. Avoid feature words.

### Rule 3 — CTA = verb + outcome, 3–5 words

Kill every "Submit", "Sign up", "Get started". Replace with `Get this week's leads`, `Show me jobs near me`, `Claim my postcode`. Trade-specific variants on trade pages.

### Rule 4 — Signup forms capped at 5 fields

For JobFilter: trade, postcode, WhatsApp, plan, pay. Anything else moves to onboarding. Put a trust marker (UK trades count, source labels) next to the button.

### Rule 5 — Mobile hero = one viewport, full pitch

Above the fold on mobile must contain: headline + sub + CTA + one proof element. Below the fold relies on scrolling — NN/g shows attention drops sharply past two screenfuls.

## Contradictions with the JobFilter brief

### "Lead quality beats UI"

- Defensible as **product** principle.
- Research-contradicted as a **landing-page** principle.
- Buyers can't evaluate lead quality pre-purchase. They decide in <7s based on hero clarity, proof, and CTA.
- **UI/copy is the proxy for lead quality at the point of conversion.**

Distinguish:
- *Product priority:* lead quality
- *Acquisition priority:* clarity + proof + CTA

### "One strong lead per week must still feel worth paying for"

- This is the right value claim.
- But it must live **inside the hero headline** — concrete, specific.
- Currently treated as internal strategy. Research says make it the headline.

Example: `One paying job a week or your money back.` That's the headline.

### Free tools / CTA implication

Free-tool pages should use the **same layer-cake structure and verb-led CTAs** as the paid funnel. Free users are the warm-up for the paid CTA — the CTA copy on free pages matters more than the brief implies.
