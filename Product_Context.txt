PART 1 — HOW TRADESMEN GET WORK & WHAT THEY HATE

The Current Reality:
UK Tradesmen rely on a mix of word-of-mouth (which is great but unpredictable) and lead-gen platforms like Checkatrade, MyBuilder, Rated People, and Bark (which are predictable but universally despised).

The Frustrations (What they hate most):

The "Race to the Bottom": Lead platforms sell the same lead to 3-5 different tradesmen. It immediately becomes a price war. They are forced to compete with "cowboys" who underbid, do shoddy work, and vanish.

Paying for Dead Leads: They pay £20-£50 just to get a phone number, only to find the homeowner is a "tyre-kicker" looking for a ballpark figure, or they just get ghosted.

The "Tyre-Kicker" Tax: Wasting fuel and 2 hours of an evening doing a site visit for someone who has a £500 budget for a £5,000 job.

Definition of a "Good Job": A good job isn't just a big job. It's a job where the client respects their expertise, values quality over the absolute cheapest price, has realistic expectations, and pays on time.

PART 2 — HOW TRADESMEN WIN OR LOSE JOBS

The Current Reality:
Quoting is the "second shift." After 8-10 hours on the tools, they spend their evenings tapping out quotes on WhatsApp, Word, or basic software like Tradify.

Why They Lose:

To Cowboys: They get undercut on price by unqualified workers.

To "Flashy" Firms: They lose high-ticket jobs (extensions, high-end landscaping, bespoke joinery) to larger, slicker firms. The larger firm might sub-contract the work out to someone worse, but the larger firm won the job because they presented a beautiful, visual, professional 3D bid deck.

Lack of Trust Signals: Homeowners are terrified of being ripped off. If a quote looks amateurish, the homeowner assumes the work will be too.

The Emotional Trigger: The burning resentment of knowing you are technically vastly superior to the guy who won the job, but losing because he had a better brochure and a cleaner quote.

PART 3 — HOW THEY MARKET THEMSELVES (THE GAPS)

The Current Reality:
Every tradesman has a smartphone filled with thousands of photos of incredible, hard-won craftsmanship.

The Gap:

The Camera Roll Graveyard: 95% of their best work dies on their phone. It never sees the light of day.

Terrible Presentation: When they do post, it's a dusty, uncropped photo of a boiler installation or a half-finished patio on a messy Facebook page with the caption "Busy week."

The "Marketing" Mental Block: They are craftsmen, not marketers. They hate the idea of "doing social media." They view marketing as BS. They just want their work to speak for itself, but they don't know how to make it speak.

PART 4 — COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

A) Lead Platforms (Checkatrade, MyBuilder)

Positioning: "Find a vetted tradesman."

Weakness: They serve the homeowner, not the tradesman. Tradesmen are just inventory. The model inherently pits tradesmen against each other.

B) Proposal / Quoting Tools (Tradify, Payaca, Fergus)

Positioning: "Save time on admin and quoting."

Weakness: They are operational tools. They create clean PDF quotes, but they don't turn technical specs into persuasive sales assets (like Codex) or high-end visual decks (like Vantage). They organize the business, but they don't necessarily win the premium jobs.

C) Marketing / Web Agencies for Trades

Positioning: "We'll build you a website and run your SEO/Facebook Ads."

Weakness: Ridiculously expensive retainers (£1k+/month). Often generate the exact same low-quality, tyre-kicker leads the platforms do, just through a different channel.

PART 5 — THE SYNTHESIS (CORE INSIGHTS)

1. What they actually want (What they mean):
They don't want "leads." They want their evenings back. They want to work exclusively for clients who don't haggle over £50. They want to stop doing site visits for time-wasters. They want the respect of being treated like a premium contractor, not a commodity.

2. What they hate most:
Being treated like a commodity by Checkatrade, and losing £50k jobs to slicker firms who do worse work.

3. What would make them instantly trust JobFilter:
Demonstrating that you understand the pain of the "tyre-kicker." If your free tools act as a barrier that actively blocks cheap homeowners from reaching their phone, they will view you as their bodyguard, not just a software vendor.

4. The "Make Me Money" realization:
They will buy when they realize: "If I turn one £20k job I normally lose into a win because my bid looked like a £5M company's bid (Vantage), and I didn't waste 10 hours quoting dead leads (Intake Engine), this system pays for itself for the next decade."
