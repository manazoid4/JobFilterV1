# SaaS Strategy & Advanced Prompting

## Strategic Thinking Partner (Advanced Prompting)

### 1. The Context Multiplier
Never rely solely on summaries. Feed raw data into the context (e.g., raw job descriptions, user logs, competitor pricing) to allow for deeper pattern recognition.

### 2. Red Team / Contrarian Follow-Up
After proposing a solution, execute a "Red Team" phase:
- Argue AGAINST your own conclusion.
- Identify blind spots and hidden risks.
- What evidence would contradict this recommendation?

### 3. The Specificity Ladder
Descend the ladder from broad goals (e.g., "improve UX") to specific, actionable components (e.g., "Reduce the padding on the primary CTA button from 1.5rem to 1rem to prevent clipping on mobile").

## SaaS Growth Frameworks

### 1. Activation Bottleneck Analysis
Identify the "Time to First Value" (TTFV). For JobFilter, this is the time from landing on the page to seeing a relevant, high-quality lead.
- **Goal**: Minimize TTFV.
- **Tactic**: Postcode-first entry, immediate scan preview.

### 2. Moat Identification
Identify where AI provides a non-commodity advantage:
- **Hidden Requirements**: Extracting nuances from job descriptions (e.g., "requires scaffolding" vs "ladder only") that standard filters miss.
- **Lead Scoring**: Proprietary multi-source correlation (e.g., Planning data + Companies House + EPC).

### 3. Progressive Disclosure
Use a modular skill structure. Load full instructions/scripts only when a specific task (e.g., "UI Refinement") is triggered.

## Implementation Bridge
End every strategic session with:
1. The **first three actions** for the immediate next phase.
2. A **30-60-90 day** roadmap.
3. A **Scenario Stress Test** (e.g., "What if a competitor releases a direct scraper tomorrow?").
